        <h1>Homegrown series nurturing tomorrow's stars </h1>
        <div class="info">
          <span class="info_l">
            By SHI FUTIAN | 
         China Daily |
            Updated: 2019-05-08 09:18
          </span>
          <span class="info_r">
            <a title="facebook" style="cursor:pointer; " onclick="javascript:bShare.share(event,'facebook',0);return false;" shape="rect"><img src="//img2.chinadaily.com.cn/static/2017www_sports/img/f_art.gif" /></a>

            <a title="linkedin" style="cursor:pointer; " onclick="javascript:bShare.share(event,'linkedin',0);return false;" shape="rect"><img src="//img2.chinadaily.com.cn/static/2017www_sports/img/in_art.gif" /></a>

          </span>
           <SCRIPT type="text/javascript" charset="utf-8" src="//static.bshare.cn/b/button.js#style=-1&amp;uuid=a92f8285-36fe-4168-8f86-8e177d015e67&amp;pophcol=2&amp;lang=en"></SCRIPT>

        <div id="Content">
          
          <figure class="image" style="display: table;"> 
 <img src="//img2.chinadaily.com.cn/images/201905/08/5cd22e79a3104842e4ac6b47.gif" data-from="newsroom" data-mimetype="image/gif" id="img-5cd22e79a3104842e4ac6b47"> 
 <figcaption style="display: table-caption; caption-side: bottom;">
   China's Jin Cheng tees off during a practice round on Tuesday for this week's PGA Tour Series-China Beijing Championship. The tour provides a vital platform for homegrown golfers to play against some of the world's best. [Photo/CHINA DAILY] 
 </figcaption> 
</figure> 
<p>Since its inception in 2014, the PGA Tour Series-China has served as a ladder for the nation's top talent to climb into the elite ranks of global golf.</p> 
<p>Now, with the domestic game really gaining a head of steam, the series is keen to reinforce its reputation as a stepping stone to greatness.</p> 
<p>Owned and operated by the PGA Tour in partnership with the China Golf Association and Shankai Sports, the series awards Web.com Tour cards to its top five money winners.</p> 
<p>In turn, the Web.com Tour feeds new talent into the PGA Tour proper.</p> 
<p>It's a proven route to stardom, which has served the likes of Li Haotong well.</p> 
<p>"I have traveled around Chinese golf courses and I have felt the people's passion and love for the sport," said Greg Carlson, executive director of the PGA Tour Series-China, in an exclusive interview with China Daily on Monday in Shanghai.</p> 
<p>"Nine players from China have won 16 of the 54 PGA Tour Series-China tournaments held over its history. To date, that's the highest percentage among all countries represented on the PGA Tour Series-China.</p> 
<p>"In addition to our expertise and brand, we provide official world ranking points, which get players into the Olympics, and we provide Web.com Tour access to further players' careers.</p> 
<p>"We are honored to be a part of growing the game of golf in China, and all of us involved in golf bear this responsibility.</p> 
<p>"We are excited to provide players in this part of the world with a competitive tour where they can improve their game."</p> 
<p>Along with Li, the PGA Tour Series-China has nurtured a number of other notable talents, including Zhang Xinjun, who won the Web.com Tour's Dormie Network Classic in San Antonio, Texas, last month to secure a return to the PGA Tour proper.</p> 
<p>Zhang and fellow PGA Tour Series-China graduate Dou Zecheng made history in 2017 when they became the first players from the Chinese mainland to earn PGA Tour cards.</p> 
<p>Back on the Web.com Tour this year, Dou won the first event of the season in the Bahamas, while Zhang has racked up four top-10 finishes.</p> 
<p>Dou and Yuan Yechun are well-positioned to join Zhang on the PGA Tour next term.</p> 
<p>Dou is currently 11th in the Web.com Tour's top 25 rankings while Yuan is just outside the qualifying section, sitting in 27th place.</p> 
<p>"I need to keep working on the same things I've been working on, which is a positive attitude on the course," Zhang said after his win in Texas.</p> 
<p>"There were times on the PGA Tour when I let my emotions get the better of me. I need to continue to grow emotionally and hopefully it'll be a new me playing out there."</p> 
<p>China's world No 35 Li remains the PGA Tour Series-China's most famous graduate.</p> 
<p>The 23-year-old, who played on the series in 2014, continues to go from strength to strength on the global stage, and last month showed he belongs at the very highest level with a solid showing in his second appearance at the Masters, playing alongside eventual winner Tiger Woods.</p> 
<p>"Li is playing a considerable amount on the PGA Tour this season but he always acknowledges his roots and pays tribute to the PGA Tour Series-China for giving him the opportunity to grow into the golfer he is today," said Carlson, who witnessed the development of the Chinese ace first-hand.</p> 
<p>"We look forward to the day he breaks through to win on the PGA Tour, knowing his professional career really took off on this tour in China."</p> 
<p>Carlson said the PGA intends to sharpen its focus on finding younger talent.</p> 
<p>"The PGA Tour Series-China operates a junior program, but we want to explore other opportunities such as junior tournaments," he said.</p> 
<p>"For kids, there's a balance they can achieve between academic life and golf. Golf can also teach them about discipline and scheduling in everyday life.</p> 
<p>"We've been cooperating with Chinese schools by having players talk to students to give them a sense of the sport. We also invite kids to golf courses to let them know the beauty of the sport."</p> 
<p>The China Golf Association (CGA) is grateful for the PGA's efforts in developing the sport here. "The PGA Tour Series-China has been a competitive and attractive international platform for Chinese golfers," said Zhang Ning, the director of the CGA's development department.</p> 
<p>"The strength of Chinese golf has been growing quickly in recent years, while many young talents who started their careers in the PGA Tour Series-China have proved their worth."</p>
